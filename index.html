About Me

My name is Mr. Montoya and I created this page to share my tips and shortcuts in mathematics. As a first-generation Latino college graduate studying mathematics I was able to witness the difference in high school math preparation amongst myself and peers. I dedicated my graduate studies to mastering the content much better than I did my first go around in undergrad. Some of these tips are based off patterns I noticed or were pointed out. Precalculus

Precalculus begins with a refresher of college algebra topics like: linear equations, absolute values, distance, area applications, quadratics, functions, transformations of functions. It then moves on to topics like: composition of functions, inverses, polynomials, rational functions, exponents, logarithms, systems of equations, sequences, series, limits, trigonometry, trigonometric formulas, and applications of trigonometry.

Calculus 1

Calculus 1 starts off by review of precalculus topics you should recall in preparation for this course. For example: library of functions, transformations, and limits. Then it starts to move into: limit laws, continuity, derivatives, derivative rules and applications, inverses of most functions, applications of differentiation, and parametric/polar equations.

Calculus 2

While calculus 1 revolves around derivatives, Calculus 2 revolves around antiderivatives aka integrals. Topics include: Riemman sums, integration rules, integration techniques, sequences, series, convergence tests. Though this list is shorter than the other sections, each topic listed unpacks with various subsections.
