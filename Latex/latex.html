Real Line

Types of Numbers

The number line consists of all real numbers (R), which can be broken down into four groups.

  1. Natural Numbers (N): aka the counting numbers are 1, 2, 3, 4, ....., ∞
  2. Integers(Z): includes the natural’s (N), their negative counterparts, and zero:  − ∞, ...,  − 2,  − 1, 0, 1, 2, ..., ∞
  3. Rationals (Q): are numbers that can be expressed as a quotient of two integers, where the denominator is not equal to zero, in other words: $\frac{p}{q}$ where p, q ∈ Z and q ≠ 0. Rationals emcompass the integers (Z) and thus the naturals (N) as well. ex: $\frac{1}{2}, -35, \frac{17}{30}, 2.75,$ etc. are Q’s.
  4. Irrationals: these are numbers that cannot be expressed as a rational. Examples can be like non-repeating decimals (like π or e) or square roots of non-perfect squares (like $\sqrt{2}, \sqrt{15},$ etc.)

Arithmetic Laws

With the above group of numbers we have the following laws: (assume a, b, c ∈ R)

Commutativity * of addition: you can add numbers in any order a + b = b + a

  • of multiplication: you can multiply numbers in any order ab = ba

Associativity * of addition: grouping doesn’t matter in addition (a + b) + c = a + (b + c)

  • of multiplication: grouping doesn’t matter in multiplication (ab)c = a(bc)

Recall PEMDAS

Always respect the order of operations, following the acronym as Parenthesis, Exponent, Multiplication or Division, Add or Subtract. Notice MD and AS have an “or”, this is because you must do whichever one comes first from left to right. For example:

9 ÷ 3(2) − 4 + 3

If done incorrectly you might do something like

9 ÷ 3(2) − 4 + 3
 = 9 ÷ 6 − 1
 = 1.5 − 1 = 0.5

But when following order from left to right you get the correct answer of:

9 ÷ 3(2) − 4 + 3
 = 3(2) − 4 + 3 (dividing the 9 by 3)
 = 6 − 4 + 3
 = 2 + 3 = 5
